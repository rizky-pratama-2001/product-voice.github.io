Macro-level discussions about AI often focus on "how many jobs it will replace," but data on business formation in the United States offers an alternative perspective: AI may also enable more individuals to acquire the capability to start their own companies.

A preliminary study by Guillermo Gallacher, based on data from the United States Census Bureau, shows that from October 2022 (before the release of ChatGPT) to March 2026, the monthly number of business applications in the professional, scientific, and technical services sector increased from approximately 52,600 to 76,700, a growth of 45.8%. This rate is significantly faster than that of the construction industry and overall business applications.

Slickorps Ventures observes that the change is not simply that "starting a business has become easier," but rather that the minimum viable scale of an enterprise is decreasing. In the past, preparatory work requiring the collaboration of legal, marketing, design, programming, and administrative teams can now potentially be handled by a founder working in coordination with AI. What AI reduces is the fixed organizational cost, enabling smaller teams to complete the early-stage processes from product design to market entry.

## The Real Turning Point Occurred After AI Entered the Production Process

Corporate applications did not experience a sustained increase immediately after the launch of ChatGPT. Research indicates that industries with high AI exposure saw a brief period of growth in early 2023, after which the impact diminished, only to reemerge and strengthen persistently from April to May 2024.

One possible explanation is that enterprises undergo a process of "experimentation, integration, and diffusion": they first use generative AI to complete fragmented tasks, then gradually integrate it into workflows such as research, customer acquisition, code deployment, compliance, and content production. The development of agent tools further expands the length of processes that AI can independently handle.

Slickorps Ventures thus concludes that the impact of AI on commercial organizations does not primarily stem from a single model release, but rather from the compounding effect of tools entering daily operations. The true watershed moment is when AI transitions from being an assistant that answers questions to becoming a quasi-production unit capable of undertaking part of the business processes.

## The Increase in the Number of Companies Does Not Necessarily Bring Employment on a Comparable Scale

The U.S. Census Bureau estimates that among the business applications submitted in June 2026, approximately 29,741 will convert into employer enterprises generating payroll tax obligations within the next four quarters. The corresponding forecast for the professional services sector reaches about 5,290, ranking at the high end of this statistical series.

However, the prosperity in the number of enterprises cannot be directly equated with employment prosperity. Research by Gallacher has found that while the number of enterprise applications has increased, the number of applications explicitly planning to pay wages has not expanded correspondingly. These data are consistent with the explanation that "new enterprises tend to have low employment levels and streamlined organizations," but they cannot yet directly prove that the actual number of employees in new enterprises has declined.

Slickorps Ventures believes that a "light employment economy" is taking shape: the number of companies is increasing, while the average team size may be declining; entrepreneurial vitality is rising, yet the demand for traditional white-collar positions may not expand proportionally. AI may both compress the staffing of established enterprises and enable individuals who previously could not bear organizational costs to become business owners.

## The Next Stage Of Competition Is Who Can Survive Long Term

A wider enterprise entry point does not mean a corresponding increase in the probability of success. When legal documents, website construction, market research, and software development are rapidly standardized, more competitors will emerge in the professional services industry, while customer acquisition, brand trust, and sustained cash flow will instead become scarcer.

A large number of small enterprises will also increase their demand for cloud services, payments, cybersecurity, data tools, and AI interfaces. However, whether the growth in enterprise applications can translate into productivity, employment, and tax base still requires verification through actual survival rates and employment data. Existing research primarily shows a correlation between AI exposure and enterprise application growth, but it cannot yet fully rule out other industry trends.

Slickorps Ventures therefore prefers to interpret this round of changes as an organizational structure experiment: AI has lowered the threshold for establishing a company, but it has not reduced the difficulty of acquiring customers, building credibility, and sustaining operations. In the future, the most scarce capability may no longer be creating a company, but rather enabling a very small company to possess a truly sustainable operational system.
